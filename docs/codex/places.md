# Places
- [PL1] Canadian Border Peak — REAL PLACE, near Chilliwack on BC–WA border. Loose metamorphic ridges, steep snow gullies. Site of Nikita’s final climb, solo and unroped. Represents culmination, quietness, and solitude.
- [PL2] Tomyhoi Peak — REAL PLACE, early joint ascent. Glacier and rocky ridge visible from Baker. Scene where Nikita forgot her helmet; narrator lent his. Day clear, no rope required. Establishes trust and lightness.
- [PL3] Mount Wedge — REAL PLACE, highest Whistler summit. Long moraine and narrow ridge. Solo climb by Nikita; late return call from Whistler around midnight. Highlights independence and fatigue.
- [PL4] Mount Baker — REAL PLACE, two-day trip May 1–2. Coleman Glacier route. Nikita ascended quickly with minimal gear. Narrator turned back at 11 a.m. due to exhaustion. Scene of contrast between light and heavy movement.
- [PL5] Forbidden Peak West Ridge — REAL PLACE, classic North Cascades line. Attempt halted at Cat Scratch Gully after assessing difficulty of fourth-class start. Illustrates discernment and restraint.
- [PL6] Whistler Village — REAL PLACE, urban counterpoint; bus terminals, coffee smell, late phone call. Bridge between mountains and ordinary life.
- [PL7] Atwell Peak — REAL PLACE, site of Chris’s fatal accident one year later. Mentioned factually, never dramatized.
- [PL8] Trailheads and camps — REAL PLACES, Squamish, Chilliwack Valley, and Boston Basin near Johannesburg Mountain and Forbidden Peak. Used to evoke preparation rituals, dawn cold, stove hiss.
